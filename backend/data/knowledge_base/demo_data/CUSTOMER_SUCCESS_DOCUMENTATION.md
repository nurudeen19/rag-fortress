## CUSTOMER ONBOARDING

### Onboarding Overview

**Objective:** Help customers achieve immediate value and successful platform launch

**Duration:** 6-8 weeks  
**Investment:** $5,000-$10,000 (included in enterprise plans)  
**Success Rate Target:** 95% of customers operational by week 8

### Pre-Sales Handoff

**Information Provided by Sales:**
- Customer name, industry, size
- Key contacts (technical, business, executive)
- Use case and expected outcomes
- Implementation timeline and priorities
- Budget approval and contract terms
- Integration requirements
- Data volume and complexity

**Pre-Kickoff Activities:**
- Onboarding specialist assigned
- Customer success manager assigned
- Launch date confirmed
- Kick-off meeting scheduled
- Implementation plan drafted

### Kickoff Meeting (Week 1)

**Attendees:**
- Customer: IT lead, business user, decision maker
- RAG Fortress: Onboarding specialist, CS manager, support engineer

**Agenda (90 minutes):**
1. Welcome and introductions (10 min)
2. Success criteria definition (15 min)
3. Implementation timeline and milestones (10 min)
4. Roles and responsibilities (10 min)
5. Platform overview and demo (25 min)
6. Data migration planning (15 min)
7. Next steps and action items (5 min)

**Outcomes:**
- Documented success criteria
- Implementation roadmap agreed
- Team contact list and communication plan
- Technical requirements finalized
- Data migration approach confirmed

### Implementation Phases (Weeks 2-6)

**Phase 1: Technical Setup (Week 2)**
- Infrastructure provisioning
- System configuration
- API integration setup
- Security and compliance review
- Customer environment readiness check

**Phase 2: Data Ingestion (Weeks 2-4)**
- Data preparation and mapping
- Migration from existing system
- Test migration in staging
- Performance optimization
- Quality validation

**Phase 3: User Training (Weeks 3-5)**
- Admin user training
- End-user training
- Power user enablement
- Documentation review
- Q&A and troubleshooting

**Phase 4: Testing and Optimization (Week 5)**
- User acceptance testing (UAT)
- Performance testing
- Integration validation
- Security testing
- Optimization for production

**Phase 5: Go-Live Preparation (Week 6)**
- Production cutover planning
- Runbook development
- Incident response plan
- Stakeholder communication
- Final pre-launch checklist

### Go-Live (Week 7)

**Go-Live Day:**
- 24/7 support team on standby
- Live monitoring and issue response
- Communication with customer
- Issue escalation if needed
- Success celebration

**Post-Launch:**
- Daily check-ins for first week
- Issue tracking and resolution
- Performance monitoring
- User feedback collection
- Optimization recommendations

### 30/60/90-Day Check-ins

**30-Day Review:**
- Platform adoption and usage
- Initial success metrics tracking
- Challenges or issues identified
- Optimization opportunities
- Training needs assessment
- Renewal conversation intro

**60-Day Review:**
- Performance metrics review
- User adoption status
- Optimization completed
- Expansion opportunities
- Next phase planning

**90-Day Review:**
- Full success criteria evaluation
- ROI and business impact analysis
- Lessons learned documentation
- Roadmap for next initiatives
- Transition to ongoing CS management

## ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT

### Customer Success Manager (CSM) Role

**Responsibilities:**
- Build trusted advisor relationships
- Understand business objectives
- Ensure platform adoption and value realization
- Drive expansion opportunities
- Monitor and prevent churn
- Executive relationship management

**Customer Segments:**

**Enterprise (>$100K ACV):**
- Dedicated CSM
- Quarterly business reviews
- Executive sponsorship
- Strategic planning
- Proactive outreach

**Mid-Market ($25-100K ACV):**
- Shared CSM (3-4 accounts)
- Semi-annual business reviews
- Regular check-ins (monthly)
- Expansion opportunities

**SMB (<$25K ACV):**
- Tech-touch/self-service
- Automated outreach campaigns
- Community support
- Self-service resources
- Email-based support

### Account Planning

**Annual Account Plan Includes:**
- Customer business objectives
- Success criteria and KPIs
- Current platform usage and adoption
- Expansion opportunities
- Risk factors and mitigation
- Quarterly touchpoint plan

### Business Review Cycle

**Quarterly Business Reviews (Enterprise):**
1. **Preparation (1 week before)**
   - Review usage data and analytics
   - Assess against success criteria
   - Identify expansion opportunities
   - Prepare slides and talking points
   - Get customer agenda in advance

2. **Meeting (60-90 minutes)**
   - Business updates and context
   - Platform usage and adoption metrics
   - ROI and value realization
   - Feature roadmap and requests
   - Expansion opportunities
   - Executive-level discussion

3. **Follow-up**
   - Send summary within 2 days
   - Outline action items and next steps
   - Update account plan
   - Schedule next review

**Topics to Cover:**
- KPI and metric achievement
- User adoption and engagement
- ROI calculation and business impact
- Integration and workflow success
- New feature utilization
- Upcoming customer needs
- Expansion or upsell opportunities
- Customer feedback and satisfaction

### Expansion and Upsell Opportunities

**Expansion Triggers:**
- Increased document volume (upgrade to higher tier)
- New departments or use cases (additional licenses)
- Advanced features needed (upgrade to premium)
- Enterprise support requirements (upgrade SLA)
- Integration needs (professional services)

**Expansion Process:**
1. **Identify Opportunity** - CSM identifies need through account analysis
2. **Prepare Business Case** - Calculate ROI and benefits
3. **Executive Alignment** - Sales director reviews and approves
4. **Customer Discussion** - Present opportunity and value
5. **Proposal** - Formal expansion proposal
6. **Negotiation** - Finalize terms
7. **Implementation** - Deploy new capabilities

### Churn Prevention

**Warning Signs:**
- Decreased feature usage
- Reduced login frequency
- Support ticket volume increase
- Executive team disengagement
- Payment issues or delays
- Competitive product inquiry
- Merger/acquisition activity

**Intervention Process:**
1. **Early Detection** - Monitor usage metrics
2. **Root Cause Analysis** - Understand the issue
3. **Engagement Plan** - Determine next steps
4. **Executive Involvement** - VP CS may contact customer
5. **Value Reinforcement** - Highlight benefits and ROI
6. **Corrective Action** - Address underlying issues
7. **Retention Offer** - Discounts or additions if appropriate

## SUPPORT AND TICKET MANAGEMENT

### Support Channels

**Email:** support@ragfortress.com  
**Chat:** In-app support chat (enterprise customers)  
**Phone:** (415) 555-0100 ext. 3300 (enterprise customers only)  
**Portal:** support.ragfortress.com (ticketing, knowledge base)  

### Ticket Severity Levels

**Severity 1 - Critical**
- Production outage or severe degradation
- Data loss or corruption
- Security vulnerability
- Multiple users impacted
- SLA: 1-hour response, 4-hour resolution target

**Severity 2 - High**
- Feature not working but with workaround
- Significant impact on productivity
- Limited users affected (1-5)
- SLA: 4-hour response, 8-hour resolution target

**Severity 3 - Medium**
- Feature working but unexpected behavior
- Limited business impact
- Single user affected
- SLA: 8-hour response, 24-hour resolution target

**Severity 4 - Low**
- Minor feature request or question
- No business impact
- General assistance
- SLA: 24-hour response, 5-day resolution target

### Ticket Lifecycle

**1. Submission**
- Customer submits through email, chat, or portal
- Auto-response sent with ticket number
- Ticket logged with details and severity

**2. Triage (L1)**
- Reviewed within SLA
- Severity assessed
- Assigned to appropriate support engineer
- Acknowledgment sent to customer

**3. Investigation (L1/L2)**
- Support engineer investigates issue
- Gathers logs and information
- Reproduces if possible
- Provides initial response

**4. Resolution (L2/L3)**
- Solution provided to customer
- Workaround offered if needed
- Customer implements fix
- Verification that issue resolved

**5. Follow-up**
- Customer confirms resolution
- Ticket closed
- Survey sent for feedback
- Knowledge base updated if needed

### Support Best Practices

**Response Quality:**
- Professional and courteous tone
- Clear and concise explanation
- Exact steps to resolve
- Screenshots or documentation when helpful
- Follow-up within 24 hours if waiting on customer

**Knowledge Base Updates:**
- Document solutions to common issues
- Create self-service articles
- Update as product changes
- Link relevant articles in ticket responses
- Maintain currency and accuracy

**Escalation Procedures:**
- Escalate if cannot resolve in 24 hours
- Contact technical lead for complex issues
- Involve product team if bug suspected
- Involve security team if vulnerability
- Update customer regularly on progress

## CUSTOMER METRICS AND REPORTING

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| KPI | Target | Frequency |
|-----|--------|-----------|
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | 110%+ | Monthly |
| Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) | 90%+ | Per ticket |
| Net Promoter Score (NPS) | 50+ | Quarterly |
| Gross Retention Rate | 95%+ | Monthly |
| Time to Value | <4 weeks | Per customer |
| Ticket Resolution Time | <24 hours | Per ticket |
| Support Response Time | <4 hours | Per ticket |

### Usage Metrics

**Tracked Per Customer:**
- Documents ingested
- Daily active users
- Searches per day
- API call volume
- Search latency (p50, p95, p99)
- Error rate
- Feature adoption

### Customer Health Scoring

**Calculation:**
- Product usage (40%): Activity level, feature adoption
- Support (20%): Ticket volume, resolution satisfaction
- Engagement (20%): QBR attendance, communication responsiveness
- Growth (20%): Account expansion, NPS, references

**Health Status:**
- **Green (Healthy):** Score 80+, low risk, growth opportunity
- **Yellow (At Risk):** Score 60-79, monitor closely, engagement needed
- **Red (Critical):** Score <60, intervention required, may churn

### Business Review Preparation

**Account Analysis Should Include:**
- YTD/YoY revenue and ARR
- Customer health score
- Usage trends and adoption
- Feature utilization
- Support ticket history
- Expansion opportunities
- Competitive threats
- Reference potential

## CUSTOMER ADVOCACY AND REFERENCES

### NPS and Satisfaction Surveys

**Post-Support Survey:**
- Sent after ticket resolution
- 1-10 rating scale plus comments
- Response rate target: 30%+
- Use feedback to improve support

**Post-Implementation Survey:**
- Sent at 30/60/90 days
- Measure success criteria achievement
- Assess satisfaction and likelihood to recommend
- Identify expansion opportunities

**Quarterly NPS Survey:**
- Sent to all customers
- Measure loyalty and likelihood to recommend
- Passive/Promoter/Detractor categorization
- Follow up with promoters and detractors

### Reference Program

**Promoter Identification:**
- NPS score 9-10
- Actively using platform
- Strong results or ROI
- Long customer tenure (12+ months)
- Willing to reference

**Reference Participation:**
- Case study interview (1 hour)
- Testimonial quote (optional)
- Reference call (30 min, 2-3 calls per year)
- Public event or webinar (optional)
- Co-marketing opportunity (optional)

**Reference Rewards:**
- Discount or credit on renewal
- Executive swag or gift
- Public recognition
- Co-authored content

### Case Studies and ROI Documentation

**Case Study Components:**
1. Customer profile and background
2. Challenge or problem statement
3. Solution implemented
4. Results and ROI achieved
5. Customer quote and testimonial
6. Key metrics and data

**Target:** 2-3 new case studies per quarter
