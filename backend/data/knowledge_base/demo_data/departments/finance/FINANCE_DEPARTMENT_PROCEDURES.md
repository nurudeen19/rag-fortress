## ACCOUNTING PRINCIPLES AND POLICIES

### Accounting Method

**Accounting Basis:** Accrual basis
- Revenue recognized when earned
- Expenses recognized when incurred
- Regardless of cash payment timing

**Chart of Accounts:**
- 5-digit account numbering system
- 10000-19999: Assets
- 20000-29999: Liabilities
- 30000-39999: Equity
- 40000-49999: Revenue
- 50000-59999: Operating Expenses
- 60000-69999: Other Income/Expense

### Key Accounting Policies

**Revenue Recognition:**
- Annual contracts: Recognized ratably over 12-month period
- Professional services: Recognized upon completion/delivery
- Subscription: Recognized monthly as service provided
- Refunds: Reversed in month issued

**Accounts Receivable:**
- Net 30 payment terms standard
- Aging report reviewed monthly
- Collection efforts after 60 days
- Write-off after 180 days past due (CFO approval)

**Accounts Payable:**
- Net 30 payment terms from vendors (standard)
- Invoice matched to PO before payment
- 1099 contractors tracked and reported annually
- Duplicate payment prevention protocols

**Expense Recognition:**
- <$500: Expensed immediately
- $500-$5,000: Depreciable assets (3-5 years)
- >$5,000: Capitalized assets (depreciation schedule)

**Depreciation:**
- Equipment: 5-year straight-line
- Furniture: 7-year straight-line
- Software licenses: 3-year straight-line
- Leasehold improvements: Life of lease or 5 years (shorter)

## FINANCIAL CONTROLS AND COMPLIANCE

### Internal Controls

**Segregation of Duties:**
- Purchases: Requester ≠ Approver ≠ Payment processor
- Payroll: Preparer ≠ Approver ≠ Processor
- Accounting: Data entry ≠ Reconciliation ≠ Reporting

**Authorization Levels:**
- Under $1,000: Department manager
- $1,000-$5,000: Director or VP
- $5,000-$25,000: CFO approval
- Over $25,000: CEO approval
- Equipment/Capital: CEO approval required

**Reconciliations:**
- Bank reconciliation: Weekly
- Credit card statements: Weekly
- General ledger accounts: Monthly
- Fixed asset register: Quarterly

### Compliance

**Regulatory Requirements:**
- Annual tax return preparation and filing (CPA firm)
- Quarterly estimated tax payments
- Payroll tax compliance and filing
- Sales tax compliance (by state)
- 1099 contractor reporting (January 31)
- W-2 employee reporting (January 31)

**Audit:**
- Annual external audit by independent CPA
- Audit field work typically January/February
- Audit report issued by March 15
- Audit committee review of findings
- Management response to audit adjustments

**Insurance and Risk:**
- Annual insurance review
- Liability coverage: $2M
- Directors and officers insurance: $3M
- Cyber liability insurance
- Workers compensation insurance (by state)

## BUDGETING AND FORECASTING

### Annual Budget Process

**Timeline:**
- **August:** Departments prepare budgets
- **September:** Finance reviews and consolidates
- **October:** Final board approval
- **November:** Budget distribution and training
- **January:** Fiscal year begins with new budget

**Budget Structure:**
- Revenue projections (by customer segment)
- Departmental operating expenses
- Headcount and salaries (locked in)
- Capex projects and investments
- Cash flow projections
- Balance sheet projections

**Department Budgeting Requirements:**
- Headcount: Number and salary levels
- Software/SaaS subscriptions
- Hardware and equipment
- Consulting and professional services
- Travel and entertainment
- Office supplies and facilities
- Marketing and advertising

**Budget Template:**
```
2025 Budget - [Department Name]

Personnel:
  Salaries & Benefits: $XXX
  Contractor/Temp: $XXX
  Total Personnel: $XXX

Operating Expenses:
  Software/SaaS: $XXX
  Hardware: $XXX
  Professional Services: $XXX
  Travel: $XXX
  Office Supplies: $XXX
  Other: $XXX
  Total Operating: $XXX

Total Budget: $XXX
```

### Variance Analysis

**Monthly Variance Report:**
- Actual spending vs. budget
- Variance by department and category
- Variance % (favorable/unfavorable)
- Explanation of significant variances
- Forecast to year-end

**Acceptable Variances:**
- ±5%: No explanation needed
- 5-10%: Brief explanation
- >10%: Detailed explanation required

**Action on Unfavorable Variances:**
- 10% unfavorable: Department review
- 15% unfavorable: VP/Director action plan
- 20% unfavorable: CEO notification and intervention

### Forecasting

**Monthly Rolling Forecast:**
- Quarterly forecast updated monthly
- 12-month outlook maintained
- Revenue forecasts updated from sales pipeline
- Expense forecasts adjusted for actuals
- Cash position projected monthly

## EXPENSE MANAGEMENT AND REIMBURSEMENT

### Expense Policy

**Eligible Expenses:**
- Business meals (client entertainment, team events)
- Travel (flights, hotels, rental cars)
- Conference and training attendance
- Software and tools for business use
- Office supplies and equipment
- Professional services (contractors, consultants)

**Ineligible Expenses:**
- Personal items or subscriptions
- Alcohol outside of business meals
- Traffic violations or parking tickets
- Personal travel insurance
- Entertainment unrelated to business
- Commuting expenses

### Expense Report Process

**Submission Requirements:**
- All receipts required for expenses >$25
- Submitted within 30 days of expense
- Business purpose documented
- Cost category selected
- Personal card/corporate card specified

**Approval Workflow:**
1. Employee submits report with receipts
2. Manager reviews and approves
3. Finance coordinator verifies compliance
4. CFO approves for payment
5. Payment processed (next payroll)

**Expense Report Template:**
| Date | Vendor | Category | Amount | Receipt | Notes |
|------|--------|----------|--------|---------|-------|
| 11/5 | Delta | Travel | $450 | Y | Flight to NY |
| 11/6 | Marriott | Travel | $150 | Y | Hotel night |
| 11/7 | Client Dinner | Meals | $75 | Y | Client meeting |

### Reimbursement Limits

**Meals:**
- Business meal solo: $20
- Team meals: $30/person
- Client entertainment: $100/person (pre-approval for $100+)
- Alcohol: Max 2 drinks per person

**Travel:**
- Flights: Economy class domestic; Business class international
- Hotels: 3-star or equivalent; $200/night max (major cities may vary)
- Rental cars: Mid-size or economy; Compact for solo
- Per diem: $50/day (instead of itemizing meals when traveling)

**Software and Tools:**
- Under $100: Approved by manager
- $100-$500: Approved by VP and Finance
- Over $500: CFO approval required

### Corporate Credit Card

**Eligible Cardholders:**
- VP level and above
- Senior individual contributors with frequent expenses
- Marketing team (for advertising)
- Sales team (for customer entertainment)

**Card Limits:**
- VP and C-level: $10,000/month
- Directors: $5,000/month
- Managers: $2,000/month
- Individual contributors: $1,000/month

**Card Usage:**
- Business expenses only
- Monthly reconciliation required
- Receipts provided within 5 days of expense
- Personal charges not permitted (fraud/termination risk)
- Card payment through expense report process

## REVENUE AND ACCOUNTS RECEIVABLE

### Revenue Recognition

**Annual Contract:**
- Contract value: $50,000
- Recognition: $50,000 / 12 = $4,167/month
- Recognized evenly throughout year

**Mid-Year Contract:**
- Contract value: $30,000 for 8 months (Aug-Mar)
- Recognition: $30,000 / 8 = $3,750/month
- Starting August through March

**Monthly Subscription:**
- Recurring charge: $5,000/month
- Recognized: In month service provided

**Professional Services:**
- Milestone 1 (Design): $10,000 - Recognized upon completion
- Milestone 2 (Implementation): $15,000 - Recognized upon completion
- Milestone 3 (Training): $5,000 - Recognized upon completion

### Invoicing and Collections

**Invoice Timing:**
- Annual contracts: Invoiced upfront (Net 30)
- Monthly subscriptions: Invoiced on 1st of month (Net 30)
- Professional services: Invoiced upon milestone completion

**Invoice Requirements:**
- Invoice number (sequential)
- Invoice date
- Customer name and address
- Service description and dates
- Amount due and payment terms
- Company bank details or payment instructions
- Purchase order (if required by customer)

**Collections Process:**
1. **Due Date:** Invoice issued Net 30
2. **30 Days Past Due:** Automated reminder email
3. **45 Days Past Due:** Phone call from AR coordinator
4. **60 Days Past Due:** AR coordinator escalation to CFO
5. **90 Days Past Due:** Legal review for collection action

**Bad Debt:**
- Provision estimated at month-end
- Write-off requires CFO approval
- Documented in accounts receivable aging
- Quarterly review for uncollectible accounts

## PAYROLL AND HUMAN RESOURCES FINANCE

### Payroll Cycle

**Schedule:**
- Bi-weekly payroll (every other Friday)
- 26 pay periods per year
- Paid on Friday; cut-off Wednesday prior

**Payroll Process:**
1. **Time Submission:** Employees submit timesheets Wednesday EOD
2. **Manager Approval:** Managers approve Thursday morning
3. **Payroll Processing:** Finance processes Friday morning
4. **Payment:** Direct deposit to employees Friday
5. **Reporting:** Payroll reports archived by pay period

**Payroll Components:**
- Gross salary/hourly wages
- Overtime (1.5x for hours >40/week)
- Bonus and commission
- PTO payouts (termination only)
- Deductions: Federal/state taxes, FICA, Social Security
- Voluntary: 401(k), FSA, HSA, dependent care FSA
- Benefits: Health insurance, life insurance

**Payroll Taxes:**
- Federal income tax: Withheld per W-4
- Social Security (6.2% employee, 6.2% employer)
- Medicare (1.45% employee, 1.45% employer)
- State income tax: Varies by state
- Unemployment insurance: Paid by employer

**Quarterly Tax Filings:**
- Form 941 (quarterly employment tax return)
- State unemployment insurance
- State income tax
- Payment via EFTPS (Electronic Federal Tax Payment System)

**Annual Tax Filings:**
- Form 940 (annual unemployment tax return)
- W-2s to employees (January 31)
- 1099s to contractors (January 31)
- Form W-3 (W-2 summary)
- Form 941-X (amended returns if needed)

### Contractor and 1099 Payments

**Contractor Eligibility:**
- Independent contractor status verified
- W-9 form on file
- No employee benefits provided
- Typically project-based or specialized work

**Payments:**
- Invoiced by contractor
- Net 30 payment terms
- Categorized by project/cost center
- Tracked separately from payroll

**Reporting:**
- 1099-MISC issued if paid >$600 in year
- Sent to contractor by January 31
- Reported to IRS and state
- Form 1096 summary filed

## FINANCIAL REPORTING

### Monthly Financial Statements

**Balance Sheet:**
- Assets (cash, receivables, equipment)
- Liabilities (payables, accrued expenses, debt)
- Equity (capital, retained earnings)
- Prepared within 10 business days of month-end

**Income Statement:**
- Revenue by product/service
- Cost of revenue (direct costs)
- Gross profit and gross margin %
- Operating expenses by category
- EBITDA and net income
- Prepared within 10 business days of month-end

**Cash Flow Statement:**
- Operating cash flow
- Investing cash flow (capex)
- Financing cash flow (debt, equity)
- Beginning and ending cash balance
- Prepared within 15 business days of month-end

### Key Financial Metrics

| Metric | Target | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Gross Margin | 75%+ | Monthly |
| EBITDA Margin | 20%+ | Monthly |
| Quick Ratio | 1.5+ | Monthly |
| Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) | <45 days | Monthly |
| Cash Runway | 12+ months | Monthly |
| Revenue Growth | 30%+ YoY | Quarterly |
| Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) | Tracking | Monthly |

### Board Reporting

**Monthly Board Report:**

- Key metrics dashboard
- Cash position and runway
- Budget variance analysis
- Headcount summary

**Quarterly Board Financials:**
- Complete financial statements

- Key metric trends

**Annual Report:**
- Audited financial statements

## FIXED ASSETS AND DEPRECIATION

### Fixed Asset Policy

**Capitalization Threshold:**
- Assets >$500 capitalized
- Assets <$500 expensed immediately

**Asset Categories:**

| Category | Life | Method | Notes |
|----------|------|--------|-------|
| Computer Equipment | 5 years | Straight-line | Laptops, desktops |
| Furniture & Fixtures | 7 years | Straight-line | Desks, chairs |
| Software Licenses | 3 years | Straight-line | Multi-user licenses |
| Leasehold Improvements | Lease life | Straight-line | Buildout, renovations |
| Vehicles | 5 years | Straight-line | Company cars |

### Asset Tracking

**Fixed Asset Register:**
- Asset name and description
- Purchase date and cost
- Asset location and user
- Accumulated depreciation
- Current book value
- Disposal date (if applicable)

**Quarterly Physical Inventory:**
- Verify all assets exist
- Confirm current location
- Identify discrepancies
- Update depreciation schedules
- Adjust for disposals

**Asset Disposal:**
- When asset no longer useful or broken
- Gain or loss calculated (cost - accumulated depreciation - salvage value)
- Recorded in accounting system
- Physical asset removed or recycled

## TAX PLANNING AND COMPLIANCE

### Income Tax Strategy

**Federal Income Tax:**
- C-Corporation (current structure)
- Quarterly estimated tax payments
- Tax accounting software (QuickBooks/Xero integration)
- Annual tax return filed by March 15

**State Income Tax:**
- File in all states where operational
- California: Franchise tax annual return
- Nexus evaluation for multi-state operations

### Tax Deductions

**Deductible Business Expenses:**
- Salaries and wages
- Employee benefits (health insurance, etc.)
- Business expenses (supplies, utilities)
- Depreciation and amortization
- Interest on business debt
- Professional services (accounting, legal)
- Meals and entertainment (50% deductible)
- Travel for business
- Marketing and advertising

**Research and Development (R&D) Tax Credit:**
- Investment in software development
- Potential tax credit eligibility
- Annual evaluation recommended
- Consider working with tax professional
