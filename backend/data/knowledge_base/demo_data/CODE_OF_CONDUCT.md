# Employee Code of Conduct

**Last Updated:** November 2025  
**Effective Date:** November 1, 2025  
**Document ID:** POL-COC-001

## Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical and professional standards expected of all employees, contractors, consultants, and representatives of our organization. This document applies to all individuals working for or representing the company in any capacity, regardless of location or employment status.

## Core Principles

### 1. Integrity and Honesty
- Conduct all business with honesty and fairness
- Be truthful in all communications with colleagues, clients, and stakeholders
- Maintain accurate and truthful records
- Admit mistakes promptly and work toward resolution
- Never falsify documents or records

### 2. Respect and Inclusivity
- Treat all individuals with dignity, courtesy, and respect
- Value and celebrate diverse perspectives, backgrounds, and experiences
- Recognize that diversity strengthens our organization
- Support an inclusive environment where everyone feels valued and heard
- Challenge biased language and behavior when observed

### 3. Professionalism
- Maintain professional demeanor in all workplace interactions
- Communicate clearly and respectfully
- Respect confidentiality of sensitive information
- Accept feedback constructively
- Model the behavior you expect from others

### 4. Accountability
- Take responsibility for your actions and decisions
- Follow established policies and procedures
- Report violations or concerns through appropriate channels
- Support organizational objectives and goals
- Be transparent about conflicts of interest

## Workplace Conduct Standards

### Professional Behavior
- Perform your duties to the best of your ability
- Arrive on time for work and scheduled meetings
- Maintain a clean and organized workspace
- Use company resources responsibly and for business purposes
- Avoid excessive personal use of company equipment and internet

### Communication Standards
- Communicate professionally in all interactions (verbal, written, digital)
- Respond to emails and messages in a timely manner (within 24 business hours)
- Use appropriate language and tone in all communications
- Avoid gossip, rumors, and negative comments about colleagues
- Address concerns or conflicts directly with involved parties first
- Escalate unresolved issues through proper channels

### Dress Code and Appearance
- Maintain appropriate grooming and hygiene
- Dress according to the standards set by your department or role
- Business casual is the standard for most positions
- Client-facing roles may require business formal attire
- Ask your manager for clarification on specific dress code requirements

## Harassment, Discrimination, and Bullying

### Zero-Tolerance Policy
Our organization maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind. This includes:

- **Discrimination:** Treating someone unfairly based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, veteran status, or any other protected characteristic
- **Harassment:** Unwelcome conduct based on protected characteristics that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment
- **Sexual Harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal/physical conduct of a sexual nature
- **Bullying:** Repeated, unreasonable behavior directed toward an individual that creates a hostile or demeaning work environment
- **Retaliation:** Any adverse action taken against someone for reporting harassment or participating in an investigation

### Reporting Harassment or Discrimination
If you experience, witness, or become aware of harassment, discrimination, or bullying:

1. **Document the incident** - Record dates, times, locations, and descriptions of what occurred
2. **Report promptly** - Report to:
   - Your direct manager, or
   - Your HR Business Partner, or
   - The Ethics and Compliance Hotline (confidential and available 24/7)
3. **Cooperate with investigations** - Provide truthful information to investigators
4. **Expect confidentiality** - Your report will be handled with appropriate discretion

All reports will be taken seriously and investigated promptly. The company is committed to protecting the confidentiality of complainants to the extent possible while conducting a thorough investigation.

## Conflicts of Interest

### Definition
A conflict of interest occurs when your personal interests could interfere with your ability to act in the company's best interests.

### Examples
- Working for a competitor or client while employed by the company
- Buying or selling services to the company at personal profit
- Accepting gifts, bribes, or favors from clients, vendors, or competitors
- Entering into business dealings with family members
- Using confidential company information for personal gain
- Holding a significant financial interest in a company that does business with us

### Disclosure and Resolution
- Disclose any potential conflicts to your manager and HR
- Provide complete and accurate information
- Work with HR to resolve conflicts appropriately
- Recuse yourself from decisions where conflicts exist
- Failure to disclose conflicts may result in disciplinary action

## Confidentiality and Information Security

### Protecting Confidential Information
- Maintain strict confidentiality of all proprietary company information
- Do not share confidential information with unauthorized parties
- Treat financial, strategic, and personnel information with utmost confidentiality
- Secure all sensitive documents and data
- Use strong passwords and change them regularly
- Never write down passwords or share login credentials

### Use of Company Data and Systems
- Use company systems, data, and resources only for authorized business purposes
- Comply with all data protection and privacy regulations
- Report security breaches or suspected unauthorized access immediately
- Respect the privacy of colleagues' information
- Follow proper protocols for data disposal
- Do not access confidential files or systems outside your area of responsibility

## Substance Abuse and Workplace Safety

### Drug and Alcohol Policy
- Report to work in a condition fit to perform your duties
- Do not use, possess, or distribute illegal drugs at work or during work hours
- Alcohol consumption is prohibited during work hours except at authorized company events
- Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol at work is prohibited
- Employees may be required to submit to drug and alcohol testing as permitted by law

### Workplace Safety
- Follow all safety procedures and use required protective equipment
- Report hazardous conditions and near-miss incidents immediately
- Cooperate with safety investigations
- Never operate equipment without proper training and authorization
- Maintain awareness of emergency evacuation procedures and meeting points

## Compliance with Laws and Regulations

### Legal Obligations
- Comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and company policies
- Report suspected illegal activity through appropriate channels
- Understand and adhere to regulations relevant to your role
- Seek guidance from HR or Legal when uncertain about compliance
- Do not engage in any illegal or unethical activities

### Anti-Corruption and Bribery
- Do not offer, give, or accept bribes in any form
- Do not offer lavish gifts to clients, vendors, or government officials
- Do not engage in illegal or improper business practices
- Report suspected corruption immediately
- Understand and comply with anti-corruption laws applicable to your region

## Disciplinary Action and Consequences

### Violation Response
Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of employment. The severity of the violation and any mitigating factors will be considered.

### Disciplinary Process
1. **Verbal Warning** - For minor first-time violations
2. **Written Warning** - For repeated or more serious violations
3. **Suspension** - For significant violations
4. **Termination** - For severe violations or repeated misconduct after warnings

### Investigation Procedure
- All reported violations will be investigated thoroughly and impartially
- The accused individual will be notified and given an opportunity to respond
- Evidence will be gathered from all relevant parties
- Decisions will be made based on facts and company policies
- The complainant will be informed of outcomes as permitted by law

### Appeals Process
- Employees may appeal disciplinary decisions through HR within 5 business days
- The appeal must include new evidence or identify procedural errors
- An impartial manager not involved in the original decision will review the appeal
- The final decision will be communicated in writing

## Acknowledgment and Training

### Mandatory Acknowledgment
All employees must acknowledge receipt and understanding of this Code of Conduct. Failure to do so may result in disciplinary action.

### Annual Training
- Employees are required to complete Code of Conduct training annually
- New employees must complete training during onboarding
- Training is mandatory regardless of prior tenure
- Attendance will be tracked and verified
