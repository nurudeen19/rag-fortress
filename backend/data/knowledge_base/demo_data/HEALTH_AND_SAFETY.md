# Workplace Health, Safety, and Emergency Procedures

**Last Updated:** November 2025  
**Effective Date:** November 1, 2025  
**Document ID:** POL-HEALTH-001

## Health and Safety Commitment

RAG Fortress is committed to maintaining a safe, healthy, and compliant workplace. We believe that a safe work environment is fundamental to our success and the well-being of all employees. This document outlines our health and safety policies and procedures.

## Workplace Safety Principles

### Zero-Incident Goal
- We aim to maintain a zero-incident workplace
- All employees are responsible for safety
- Reporting hazards is encouraged and expected
- Safety is never compromised for productivity
- Continuous improvement of safety practices

### Safety Responsibilities

**Management Responsibility:**
- Provide safe working conditions
- Ensure safety equipment and procedures are available
- Investigate all incidents and near-misses
- Communicate safety expectations clearly
- Enforce safety policies consistently

**Employee Responsibility:**
- Follow all safety procedures and wear required protection
- Report hazards immediately
- Participate in safety training
- Never disable or bypass safety equipment
- Report all incidents, no matter how minor

**Individual Responsibility:**
- Work safely and look out for coworkers
- Ask questions if unsure about procedures
- Avoid shortcuts that compromise safety
- Be mentally alert and avoid distractions
- Take care of your own health and wellness

## Hazard Communication and Reporting

### Identifying Hazards
Employees should be aware of potential workplace hazards including:
- Electrical hazards
- Ergonomic strain
- Slips, trips, and falls
- Fire hazards
- Chemical hazards
- Machinery and equipment hazards
- Environmental hazards

### Reporting Procedures
1. **Immediately Report** potential hazards to your manager or safety representative
2. **Document the Hazard** - Describe location, nature, and severity
3. **Avoid the Area** if safe to do so; use caution tape if necessary
4. **Facilities Inspection** - Facilities will investigate and remedy
5. **Follow-Up** - Confirm that hazard has been resolved

### Near-Miss Reporting
- Near-miss incidents should be reported even if no injury occurred
- Near-miss reports help prevent future incidents
- Reports are confidential and not used for discipline
- Reporting is encouraged and appreciated
- Monthly review of near-miss reports for pattern identification

## Ergonomics and Workstation Safety

### Office Ergonomics
- Workstations are assessed for proper ergonomic setup
- Monitor at eye level, approximately 20-26 inches away
- Keyboard and mouse at elbow height
- Chair with lower back support
- Feet flat on floor or footrest
- Document holders at eye level

### Adjustments and Equipment
- Adjustable standing desks available upon request
- Ergonomic keyboards and mice available
- Monitor arms and stands for positioning
- Footrests and cushions available
- Wrist rests for keyboard and mouse support

### Strain Prevention
- Change positions frequently throughout the day
- Take 5-minute breaks every hour
- Perform neck and shoulder stretches
- Use proper technique when lifting (bend at knees, not back)
- Report pain or discomfort immediately

### Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI) Prevention
- Vary tasks to avoid repetitive motions
- Maintain proper posture and technique
- Take regular breaks from computer work
- Strengthen and stretch hands and wrists regularly
- Report early symptoms (numbness, tingling, pain)

## Fire Safety and Emergency Procedures

### Fire Prevention
- Keep all exits clear and unobstructed
- Do not block fire extinguishers, alarms, or sprinklers
- Do not obstruct hallways with equipment or materials
- Report damaged fire safety equipment immediately
- Maintain safe distance from heating equipment

### Fire Safety Equipment
- Fire extinguishers located on each floor (see posted maps)
- Pull stations located near all exits
- Sprinkler systems throughout building
- Fire suppression system in server room
- Emergency lighting and exit signs throughout

### Fire Extinguisher Use (PASS Method)
1. **Pull** the pin at the top of the extinguisher
2. **Aim** the nozzle at the base of the fire
3. **Squeeze** the handle to release the contents
4. **Sweep** side-to-side to cover the fire base

### Fire Alarm Response
- Immediately evacuate when alarm sounds
- Use stairs only (never elevators)
- Close doors behind you as you exit
- Proceed to the designated assembly point (see map)
- Wait for all-clear before re-entering building
- Report any stragglers to the incident commander

### Building Evacuation Procedures
**Assembly Points:**
- **San Francisco HQ:** Parking lot across the street
- **New York Office:** City park to the north
- **London Office:** Public plaza adjacent to building

**Evacuation Routes:**
- Multiple stairwells on each floor
- Posted evacuation maps at all exits
- Emergency lighting guides the way
- Designated meeting areas for each department

**Special Evacuations:**
- **Persons with Mobility Issues:** Designated areas of refuge; assist if possible
- **Persons with Hearing Impairment:** Visual alarms and text alerts provided
- **Persons Needing Assistance:** Identify yourself to emergency responders

### Emergency Alert System
- SMS/Email alerts for severe weather and incidents
- Opt-in to emergency communications during onboarding
- Update contact information as needed
- Employees directed to company emergency hotline for updates

## First Aid and Medical Emergencies

### First Aid Training
- First aid training available to all employees
- CPR/AED certification courses offered quarterly
- Advanced first aid courses available
- Refresher training every 2 years recommended
- Training location: HR Department, Building A

### First Aid Response
1. **Call Emergency Services** (911 in US) - Provide location and description
2. **Move to Safe Area** if possible without risk
3. **Apply First Aid** if trained and comfortable
4. **Provide Comfort** - Stay with injured person until help arrives
5. **Document Incident** - Complete incident report afterward

### Automatic External Defibrillators (AED)
- AED devices located on each floor (see posted maps)
- Accessible 24/7 for emergencies
- Training provided on AED use
- Anyone can use AED - no special training required
- After use, inform facilities for device servicing

### Accident and Injury Reporting
- **Report Immediately** - Inform manager and HR
- **Seek Medical Attention** - Get treatment if injured
- **Complete Incident Report** - File within 24 hours
- **Workers' Compensation** - Available for work-related injuries
- **Investigation** - HR will investigate all incidents

### Workers' Compensation
- Available for all work-related injuries and illnesses
- Medical treatment covered with approved provider
- Wage replacement for missed time (typically 60-80% of salary)
- Rehabilitation services available
- Return-to-work program for modified duty

### Returning to Work
- Light duty assignment available if approved by doctor
- Gradual return schedule possible for serious injuries
- Workplace modifications made as medically necessary
- Job protection during recovery period
- Discuss with manager and HR about timeline

## Incident Investigation and Reporting

### Incident Classification
- **Near-Miss:** Potential incident with no injury or damage
- **Minor Incident:** First aid treatment only, no lost time
- **Lost-Time Incident:** Results in absence from work
- **Serious Incident:** Requires hospitalization or medical leave
- **Fatality:** Loss of life (federal investigation required)

### Investigation Process
1. Secure the scene and provide medical attention if needed
2. Separate involved parties for individual interviews
3. Gather evidence and documentation
4. Identify root causes (not just immediate causes)
5. Develop corrective actions to prevent recurrence
6. Communicate findings and actions to involved parties
7. Track corrective actions to completion

### Incident Documentation
- Incident report form completed within 24 hours
- Witness statements collected and documented
- Photos or video of scene preserved
- All corrective actions recorded
- Follow-up verification of effectiveness
- Records maintained for 5 years minimum
