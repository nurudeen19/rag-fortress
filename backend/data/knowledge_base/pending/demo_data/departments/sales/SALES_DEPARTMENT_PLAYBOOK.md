## SALES PROCESS

### The RAG Fortress Sales Cycle

**Phase 1: Lead Generation and Prospecting (1-2 weeks)**

**Objective:** Identify and qualify potential customers

**Activities:**
- Inbound lead qualification (marketing leads)
- Outbound prospecting (cold outreach)
- Industry research and targeting
- LinkedIn prospecting and connection
- Event and conference follow-up

**Qualification Criteria (BANT):**
- **B**udget: Does company have budget for solution?
- **A**uthority: Are we talking to decision maker or influencer?
- **N**eed: Does prospect have clear problem we solve?
- **T**imeline: When do they need solution implemented?

**Tools:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator for prospecting
- Hunter.io for email discovery
- Apollo.io for outbound sequences
- Company research via LinkedIn, G2, industry reports

**Metrics:**
- Calls per day: 30-50
- Emails per day: 100-150
- Meetings scheduled: 4-6 per week
- Pipeline generated: $100K+ per month

---

**Phase 2: Initial Discovery Call (20-30 minutes)**

**Objective:** Understand prospect needs and determine fit

**Structure:**
1. **Introduction (2 min):** Introduce yourself and company
2. **Purpose (1 min):** "I wanted to learn about how you're currently managing..."
3. **Discovery Questions (15 min):** Ask about their situation
4. **Qualification (5 min):** Determine if good fit
5. **Next Steps (2 min):** Schedule follow-up if qualified

**Discovery Questions to Ask:**
- "What are your main challenges with document management today?"
- "How are you currently handling knowledge retrieval?"
- "What's your current search process?"
- "How many documents do you need to manage?"
- "Who else on your team needs to be involved?"
- "What's your timeline for implementing a solution?"

**Red Flags (Not Qualified):**
- No identified problem or need
- Won't talk to IT or decision maker
- Already committed to competitor
- Budget not available
- Too long timeline (18+ months)

**Desired Outcomes:**
- Scheduled discovery call with all stakeholders
- Clear understanding of their challenges
- Permission to do research for proposal
- Added to pipeline

---

**Phase 3: Needs Analysis and Discovery (1-2 weeks)**

**Objective:** Deeply understand prospect's business and technical requirements

**Activities:**
- Meeting with stakeholders (IT, business users, decision maker)
- Understand current state and pain points
- Learn about their technical environment
- Understand budget and decision process
- Map solution to their specific needs

**Stakeholder Roles (Typical):**
- **Technical Champion:** IT director or CTO (understands technical needs)
- **Business Champion:** Department head (feels pain of current solution)
- **Economic Buyer:** CFO or VP (controls budget)
- **User:** End user of the solution

**Key Questions:**
- How many documents need to be searchable?
- What systems do you need to integrate with?
- What's your current search infrastructure?
- How many concurrent users?
- What's your data security requirements?
- What's your implementation timeline?
- What's your budget range?

**Deliverables:**
- Discovery meeting notes
- Documented customer requirements
- Technical environment understanding
- Budget and timeline confirmation

---

**Phase 4: Solution Proposal (1-2 weeks)**

**Objective:** Present tailored solution that addresses prospect needs

**Proposal Components:**
1. **Executive Summary** (1 page)
   - Business problem and impact
   - Proposed solution overview
   - Expected outcomes and ROI
   
2. **Current State Analysis** (1-2 pages)
   - Documented challenges
   - Current state challenges
   - Risks of status quo
   
3. **Proposed Solution** (2-3 pages)
   - Solution overview
   - Technical architecture
   - Implementation timeline
   - Expected outcomes
   
4. **Pricing and Terms** (1 page)
   - Solution cost breakdown
   - Implementation services cost
   - Ongoing support costs
   - Payment terms
   
5. **Implementation Plan** (1-2 pages)
   - Phased approach
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Team and resources
   - Success metrics
   
6. **About RAG Fortress** (1 page)
   - Company overview
   - Customer references
   - Support and success team
   - Security and compliance

**Proposal Pricing Model:**

**Annual License Fee:** Based on usage tier
- **Tier 1:** $25K/year (up to 50GB documents, 100 concurrent users)
- **Tier 2:** $50K/year (up to 200GB documents, 500 concurrent users)
- **Tier 3:** $100K/year (up to 1TB documents, unlimited users)
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing (enterprise features, SLA)

**Implementation Services:** (Optional)
- Data migration: $2,500-$10,000 (based on size)
- Custom integrations: $5,000-$50,000 (based on complexity)
- Training and enablement: $3,000-$10,000 (based on users)

**Payment Terms:**
- Annual: Net 30
- Multi-year discounts: 10% (3-year), 15% (5-year)
- Implementation: 50% upfront, 50% on completion

---

**Phase 5: Negotiation and Closing (1-4 weeks)**

**Objective:** Resolve objections and close the deal

**Common Objections and Responses:**

**Objection: "Your price is too high"**
- Response: Compare TCO vs. competitor or current state
- Highlight unique value and ROI
- Discuss payment flexibility or pilot approach

**Objection: "We need to evaluate more vendors"**
- Response: "Of course. What additional information can I provide?"
- Offer reference call with similar customer
- Provide case study and comparison matrix

**Objection: "We need approval from IT"**
- Response: "Great! Would it help if I presented to your IT team?"
- Offer technical deep-dive with technical team
- Provide security/compliance documentation

**Objection: "We need to think about it"**
- Response: "Absolutely. What specific questions can I answer to help?"
- Set clear follow-up meeting
- Provide summary email with key points

**Closing Techniques:**

**Trial Close:** "If we could address your concern about X, would you be ready to move forward?"

**Assumptive Close:** "Let's plan for a September implementation. Does that work with your timeline?"

**Alternative Close:** "Would you prefer the Tier 2 or Tier 3 plan?"

**Direct Close:** "Based on everything we've discussed, I believe this is the right solution for your team. I'd like to move forward and get your signature on the agreement."

**Negotiation Tactics:**
- Focus on value, not just price
- Get decision maker involved early
- Understand their constraints and flexibility
- Package value (include services or discounts in lieu of price cuts)
- Know your walkaway price
- Build urgency with limited-time offers

**Closing Checklist:**
- [ ] All stakeholders agree on solution and pricing
- [ ] Budget approved and secured
- [ ] Decision maker signing authority confirmed
- [ ] Implementation timeline agreed
- [ ] Contract reviewed and signed
- [ ] Payment terms agreed
- [ ] Support plan outlined

### Sales Pipeline Stages

**1. Prospect** (Unqualified Lead)
- New inbound or outbound lead
- Not yet qualified
- Time in stage: 1 week
- Action: Qualify or disqualify
- Probability: 10%

**2. Qualified** (BANT Confirmed)
- Lead meets BANT criteria
- Clear business need identified
- Budget exists or likely
- Timeline identified (next 12 months)
- Time in stage: 2 weeks
- Action: Schedule discovery meeting
- Probability: 30%

**3. Discovery** (Needs Understanding)
- Discovery call(s) completed
- Customer's challenges documented
- Solution need validated
- Stakeholders identified
- Time in stage: 2 weeks
- Action: Prepare proposal
- Probability: 40%

**4. Proposal/Demo** (Solution Presentation)
- Proposal sent or demo presented
- Customer reviewing solution
- Questions being addressed
- Competitor evaluation ongoing
- Time in stage: 2-4 weeks
- Action: Address objections, schedule next review
- Probability: 50%

**5. Negotiation** (Deal Terms Discussion)
- Final pricing/terms being negotiated
- Contract review with legal
- Budget confirmation
- Final stakeholder approvals pending
- Time in stage: 1-4 weeks
- Action: Close objections, get signatures
- Probability: 70%

**6. Closed Won** (Contract Signed)
- Contract fully executed
- Payment received (or scheduled)
- Implementation begins
- Opportunity closed as won
- Time in stage: 0 (end of cycle)
- Action: Transition to Customer Success
- Probability: 100%

**7. Closed Lost** (Deal Failed)
- Customer chose competitor
- Budget cut or timeline extended beyond sales cycle
- Customer no longer interested
- Opportunity closed as lost
- Time in stage: 0 (end of cycle)
- Action: Plan for future opportunities, note lessons learned
- Probability: 0%

## COMPENSATION AND COMMISSION

### Account Executive Compensation

**Base Salary + Commission Model:**
- **Base Salary:** $80,000 - $100,000 (based on experience)
- **Target On-Target Earnings (OTE):** $150,000 - $175,000
- **Commission:** 25% of base salary annually

**Commission Structure:**
- Commission earned on annual contract value (ACV)
- Commission paid monthly on new logos and expansions
- Paid in month following contract signature
- Capped at 1.5x commission above quota (rare)

**Quota:**
- Annual Quota: $500K - $750K ACV (based on territory)
- Monthly targets: Quota / 12
- Q-level compensation adjustment at 100% + quota payout

**Commission Payout Examples:**
- $50K ACV deal = $12,500 commission (25% * $50K)
- $100K ACV deal = $25,000 commission (25% * $100K)
- Paid monthly: $50K ACV spread = $4,167/month for 12 months

**Commission Accelerators:**
- 100% Quota: Standard 25% commission
- 110-125% Quota: 30% commission (accelerator kicks in)
- 125%+ Quota: 35% commission (max commission rate)

## SALES BEST PRACTICES

### Prospecting Tips

1. **Quality over Quantity**
   - Target companies with strong fit
   - Research before outreach
   - Personalize all communications

2. **Multi-Channel Outreach**
   - Combine email, phone, LinkedIn
   - Wait 3-5 days between attempts
   - Mix of content and relationship-building

3. **Persistence**
   - Average 5-7 touches before response
   - Don't give up too early
   - Professional and value-focused

4. **LinkedIn Best Practices**
   - Complete, professional profile photo
   - Customized connection requests (avoid default message)
   - Engage with prospect content before outreach
   - Share valuable content regularly

### Discovery Call Best Practices

1. **Prepare Thoroughly**
   - Research company and prospect
   - Prepare 5-7 questions
   - Have specific talking points ready
   - Know your value proposition cold

2. **Ask More Than You Tell**
   - Ask open-ended questions
   - Let prospect talk (60% of call)
   - Listen more than pitch
   - Take detailed notes

3. **Discover Pain**
   - Understand business impact of their problem
   - Quantify the pain (cost, time, resources)
   - Understand their priorities
   - Map to your solution

4. **Next Steps are Critical**
   - Clarify next steps before call ends
   - Schedule follow-up immediately
   - Get decision maker involved
   - Send recap email with next steps

### Demo Best Practices

1. **Customize the Demo**
   - Show features relevant to their needs
   - Use their data/use case if possible
   - Skip irrelevant features
   - Keep demo 20-30 minutes

2. **Focus on Value**
   - Tie features to their stated problems
   - Show business impact, not just features
   - Use "Magic Moments" (most impressive features)
   - Provide a/b comparison to current state

3. **Engage Prospect**
   - Ask questions during demo
   - Let them try features if possible
   - Address questions immediately
   - Gauge interest and questions

4. **Call to Action**
   - Clear next steps at end of demo
   - Propose pilot or proof of concept
   - Get stakeholder commitment
   - Schedule follow-up meeting
