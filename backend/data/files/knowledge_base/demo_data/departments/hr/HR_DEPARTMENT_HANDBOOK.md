## RECRUITMENT AND HIRING

### Recruitment Process Overview

**Stage 1: Requisition and Job Posting (2-3 days)**
- Department head submits headcount request with business justification
- HR reviews and approves requisition
- Job description created or updated
- Internal job posting posted (5 days minimum)
- External posting if no internal candidates
- Salary range determined and approved

**Stage 2: Screening and Initial Interview (1-2 weeks)**
- Applications reviewed by recruiting coordinator
- Qualified candidates identified
- Phone/video screen conducted (30 minutes)
- Screening interview notes documented
- Top candidates identified for next round

**Stage 3: In-Person Interviews (1-2 weeks)**
- Typically 2-3 rounds of interviews
- Panel includes: hiring manager, peer, senior leader
- Interviews assess: technical skills, culture fit, communication
- Interview feedback documented on scorecard
- Candidates given tour and Q&A opportunity
- Timeline for next steps communicated

**Stage 4: Assessment and Reference Checks (3-5 days)**
- Technical assessment if applicable (for engineering/technical roles)
- Reference checks (3 references minimum)
- Background check initiated
- Final decision made by hiring manager and HR

**Stage 5: Offer and Negotiation (2-3 days)**
- Written offer prepared by HR
- Offer includes: salary, benefits, start date, title
- Candidate given 24-48 hours to accept
- Upon acceptance, background check completed
- Employment agreement signed

**Stage 6: Onboarding Preparation (1 week)**
- Welcome email sent to new hire
- Onboarding materials prepared
- Equipment ordered (laptop, phone, etc.)
- System access provisioned
- Buddy assigned from team

### Job Posting Requirements

All job postings must include:
- **Job Title** - Clear and descriptive
- **Department** - Where the role sits in organization
- **Reporting Structure** - Who the person reports to
- **Location** - On-site, remote, hybrid
- **Salary Range** - Published salary range
- **Job Summary** - 2-3 sentence overview
- **Responsibilities** - 5-8 key responsibilities
- **Requirements** - Must-have qualifications
- **Preferred Qualifications** - Nice-to-have skills
- **Benefits Overview** - Key benefits and perks
- **Application Instructions** - How to apply
- **EEO Statement** - Equal opportunity statement

### Interviewing Best Practices

**Before Interview:**
- Review candidate resume thoroughly
- Prepare specific questions related to role
- Set up interview space (quiet, comfortable)
- Have water available for candidate
- Test video/audio if remote interview

**During Interview:**
- Start on time, give candidate full attention
- Introduce yourself and outline interview structure
- Ask prepared questions consistently across candidates
- Take notes on candidate responses
- Allow candidate time to ask questions
- Explain next steps and timeline clearly
- Be friendly and professional

**After Interview:**
- Complete feedback form within 24 hours
- Rate candidate on scorecard
- Note specific examples and observations
- Provide feedback to recruiting coordinator
- Communicate timeline for next round

### Hiring Manager Responsibilities

- Identify business need and create job description
- Participate in interview process (typically 1-2 rounds)
- Review candidate qualifications and make recommendation
- Participate in offer negotiation if needed
- Prepare team for new hire arrival
- Plan onboarding and training schedule
- Conduct first-week check-in

### Hiring Timeline and Expectations

- **Small roles (individual contributors):** 2-3 weeks
- **Experienced roles (mid-level):** 3-4 weeks
- **Leadership roles (manager+):** 4-6 weeks
- **Specialized/hard-to-fill roles:** 6-8 weeks

Delays are common due to candidate availability, reference check delays, background screening, or competitive offers.

### Offer Letter Components

**Standard offer letter includes:**
- Job title and reporting relationship
- Start date
- Employment type (full-time, part-time, contract)
- Salary and payment frequency
- Bonus structure if applicable
- Benefits overview and start date
- At-will employment statement
- Contingency on background check
- Signature lines for candidate and employer

### Background Check Process

- **Screening Level:** Conducted on all final candidates
- **Timeframe:** 3-5 business days
- **Components:** Criminal history, employment verification, education verification, credit (financial roles only)
- **Costs:** Paid by company
- **Offer Contingency:** Job offer contingent on clear background check
- **Disputes:** Candidate can dispute inaccuracies; company investigates

### Making the Offer

**Offer Call (HR or Hiring Manager):**
1. Express enthusiasm about the opportunity
2. Discuss salary, benefits, and start date
3. Answer candidate questions
4. Get verbal acceptance
5. Send written offer immediately
6. Request signed offer by specific date

**Verbal Offer Script Example:**
"We're excited to offer you the [Position] role at RAG Fortress! The salary is $[X], benefits begin on day one, and we'd like you to start on [Date]. We have a great team and believe you'll be an excellent fit. Do you have any questions?"

## ONBOARDING AND NEW HIRE INTEGRATION

### Onboarding Objectives
- New hire feels welcomed and excited
- Completes required paperwork and training
- Understands role expectations and performance metrics
- Meets team members and key contacts
- Is productive and confident by end of first month

### Pre-Arrival Tasks (1 week before start)

**By Hiring Manager:**
- Send welcome email with start date, time, location
- Provide parking/transit information if on-site
- Share schedule for first day
- Assign team buddy or mentor
- Set up workspace (desk, equipment, supplies)

**By IT:**
- Create email account and employee systems access
- Set up laptop with software and configurations
- Create phone extension
- Add to necessary distribution lists
- Prepare access badges and credentials

**By HR:**
- Prepare onboarding materials package
- Create personnel file
- Set up benefits administration
- Prepare welcome packets
- Schedule first-day orientation

### First Day Schedule

**Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM):**
- 8:45 AM: Arrive, meet facilities coordinator
- 9:00 AM: HR orientation (30 minutes) - welcome, building tour, parking, emergency procedures
- 9:30 AM: IT setup (30 minutes) - laptop, phone, access cards, passwords
- 10:00 AM: Hiring manager onboarding (30 minutes) - role overview, expectations, team intro
- 10:30 AM: Team introductions and coffee (30 minutes)
- 11:00 AM: Workspace setup and documents

**Lunch (12:00 PM - 1:00 PM):**
- Team lunch (paid by company)

**Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
- 1:00 PM: Benefits orientation (30 minutes) - health insurance, 401(k), HSA options
- 1:30 PM: Policy overview (30 minutes) - code of conduct, confidentiality, harassment policy
- 2:00 PM: I-9 verification and background check consent
- 2:30 PM: Meet with HR business partner
- 3:00 PM: Rest of afternoon with manager - role details, first projects, questions

### First Week Activities

**Day 2:**
- Team meetings and project introductions
- System access verification
- Workspace tour and supplies
- Introduction to cross-functional partners

**Day 3:**
- Department meeting (if applicable)
- Process training (CRM, project management tools, etc.)
- Lunch with team or mentoring group
- Start assigned projects or training

**Day 4-5:**
- Full integration into team
- Training on company systems and tools
- Begin actual work on assigned projects
- Meet with other department heads
- Complete required training modules (online)

### First 30 Days

**Week 2-4:**
- Daily 1:1 check-ins with hiring manager (first 2 weeks)
- Weekly 1:1 meetings after first 2 weeks
- Integration into team meetings and projects
- Lunch meetings with different teams
- Learning about company culture and processes
- Attend department meeting (if applicable)
- Complete all mandatory training courses
- Set initial performance goals

**30-Day Check-In:**
- Feedback on how onboarding went
- How is the role matching expectations?
- Any concerns or questions?
- Adjustments needed?
- Schedule for next 60 days

### Mandatory Training (All New Hires)

**Required in First Month:**
1. Code of Conduct training (1 hour)
2. Workplace Safety and Emergency Procedures (30 minutes)
3. Anti-Harassment and Discrimination Policy (1 hour)
4. Data Security and Confidentiality (1 hour)
5. Role-Specific Training (varies by department)

### 90-Day Probationary Period

**Purpose:** Assess fit and performance before full employment commitment

**Timeline:**
- Days 1-30: Integration and foundational knowledge
- Days 31-60: Increased responsibility and project involvement
- Days 61-90: Independent work and full contribution

**30-Day Review (Week 4):**
- Feedback on adaptation and performance
- Expectations met? Any concerns?
- Adjustments to role or support needed?
- Continue to full employment (standard path)

**60-Day Review (Week 8):**
- Significant progress on projects?
- Team collaboration effective?
- Meeting performance expectations?
- Continue to full employment or adjust

**90-Day Review (Week 12):**
- Final assessment of probationary period
- Performance met/exceeds expectations?
- Continue to full employment (ongoing)
- End of probationary period

**Performance Standards:**
- Attendance and punctuality met
- Completes assigned work
- Learns company systems and processes
- Demonstrates cultural fit
- Communicates and collaborates effectively

**Outcomes:**
- **Standard Path:** Continue employment with no conditions
- **Extended Probation:** Additional 30-60 days if needed
- **Termination:** Rarely used but possible if severe performance issues

## EMPLOYEE RELATIONS AND SUPPORT

### Employee Relations Role

HR Business Partners serve as employee advocates and support employee well-being:
- One-on-one coaching and support
- Career development conversations
- Conflict resolution and mediation
- Performance management support
- Benefits and policy guidance
- Work-life balance initiatives

### Conflict Resolution Process

**Stage 1: Direct Conversation (Immediate)**
- Employees encouraged to resolve directly with person involved
- Manager available to facilitate if needed
- Goal: Reach understanding and resolution
- Documentation only if unresolved after conversation

**Stage 2: Manager Involvement (24-48 hours if not resolved)**
- Employee or affected party brings issue to manager
- Manager meets with involved parties separately
- Gathers facts and context
- Attempts to facilitate resolution
- Documents conversation and agreed resolution

**Stage 3: HR Involvement (If still unresolved)**
- Employee escalates to HR Business Partner
- HR conducts mediated discussion
- Facilitates agreement or documents unresolved issues
- May recommend additional support or training
- Documents outcome in employee file

**Stage 4: Formal Investigation (If allegations of misconduct)**
- HR director involved
- Independent investigation conducted
- Interviews with all parties and witnesses
- Evidence gathered and reviewed
- Findings documented
- Appropriate action taken based on findings

### Performance Issues and Improvement Plans

**Performance Concerns Trigger:**
- Failure to meet job requirements
- Attendance or tardiness issues
- Quality or quantity of work problems
- Behavioral or conduct issues
- Not meeting performance objectives

**Performance Improvement Plan (PIP):**
- Formal document outlining specific concerns
- Clear, measurable objectives for improvement
- Timeline for improvement (typically 30-90 days)
- Resources and support provided
- Regular check-ins with manager
- Consequences if not met

**PIP Components:**
1. **Issue Description** - Specific performance concerns
2. **Impact** - How issue affects team/company
3. **Expectations** - What needs to improve
4. **Metrics** - How success will be measured
5. **Timeline** - When improvement needs to occur
6. **Support** - Resources, training, coaching provided
7. **Check-In Schedule** - Frequency of feedback (weekly or bi-weekly)
8. **Consequences** - What happens if PIP not met

**PIP Process:**
- Manager and HR develop plan together
- Presented to employee with HR present
- Employee given time to ask questions
- Goal is improvement (not termination path)
- Regular feedback and support provided
- Reassessment at end of period

## BENEFITS ADMINISTRATION

### Health Insurance

**Plans Offered:**
- **Medical:** Multiple PPO and HMO plans at different coverage levels
- **Dental:** Preventive, basic, and major coverage
- **Vision:** Eye exams, glasses, contacts coverage

**Enrollment:**
- New hires: Enroll first day; coverage starts immediately
- Annual: Open enrollment in October (coverage Jan 1)
- Qualifying life event: 30 days to enroll

**Costs:**
- **Medical:** Company pays 85% individual; 75% family
- **Dental:** Company pays 85%
- **Vision:** Company pays 100%

**Claiming Benefits:**
- Use provided insurance cards
- In-network providers for best rates
- Employee pays deductible, then insurance covers
- Claims processed by insurance company (typically 7-14 days)

### 401(k) Retirement Plan

**Plan Features:**
- Pre-tax contributions (reduce taxable income)
- Company match: 4% of salary (company pays 4%, you contribute 4% minimum to receive)
- Vesting: Immediate for employee contributions; 1-year vesting for company match
- Investment options: 15+ mutual funds at different risk levels

**Enrollment:**
- New hire: Enroll first day if desired
- Default: Not auto-enrolled (must opt-in)
- Contribution rate: 1-50% of salary (minimum $50/paycheck)

**Company Match:**
- Company contributes 4% of salary for eligible employees
- Contribution made quarterly
- Automatic if you contribute at least 4%

**Withdrawals:**
- Cannot withdraw before age 59Â½ (with exceptions)
- Roth conversions available
- Loans from 401(k) available (ask HR)

### Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)

**Medical FSA:**
- Save on tax-free medical expenses
- Contribute up to $3,300/year (2025)
- Use for: copays, deductibles, prescriptions, dental, vision
- Must use it or lose it (no rollover, except $610 carryover)

**Dependent Care FSA:**
- Save on tax-free childcare expenses
- Contribute up to $5,000/year (married filing jointly)
- Use for: daycare, preschool, after-school programs
- Must use it or lose it

**Enrollment:**
- Annual enrollment in October
- Effective Jan 1
- Life event changes allowed outside enrollment window

### Health Savings Account (HSA)

**Eligibility:**
- Must be enrolled in high-deductible health plan (HDHP)
- Cannot be covered by other health insurance
- Cannot be claimed as dependent on other return

**Contribution Limits:**
- Individual: $4,150/year (2025)
- Family: $8,300/year (2025)
- Must open account with approved provider

**Tax Advantages:**
- Tax-free contributions
- Tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses
- Tax-free growth
- Can roll over unused funds year-to-year

**Use:**
- Debit card provided
- Pay for: prescriptions, copays, deductibles, medical equipment
- Keep receipts for IRS substantiation

### Life Insurance

**Company-Paid Coverage:**
- Life insurance = 2x your annual salary
- Automatically provided (no cost to you)
- Beneficiary designation required (set up during onboarding)

**Supplemental Coverage:**
- Additional life insurance available for purchase
- Options: 1x, 2x, 3x, or 4x salary
- Rates based on age and health
- Can enroll during annual enrollment

**Claims Process:**
- Beneficiary contacts HR with death certificate
- HR initiates claim with insurance company
- Payment typically within 10-15 business days

### Disability Insurance

**Short-Term Disability (STD):**
- Covers 60-80% of salary
- Waiting period: 7-14 days
- Benefit duration: Up to 13 weeks

**Long-Term Disability (LTD):**
- Covers 60% of salary
- Waiting period: After STD ends (typically 13 weeks)
- Benefit duration: Until retirement age (typically 65)

**Filing Claim:**
- Contact HR for claim form
- Provide medical documentation
- HR submits to insurance company
- Company holds job during approved leave

### Paid Time Off

**Annual PTO:** 15 days (accrued 1.25 days/month)
**Sick Leave:** 8 days/year (taken as needed)
**Holidays:** 10 federal + 2 floating
**Parental Leave:** 4 weeks (primary), 2 weeks (secondary)
**Bereavement Leave:** 3-5 days depending on relation

### Open Enrollment

**Timing:** October annually

**Changes Allowed:**
- Switch health insurance plans
- Add/remove dependents
- Adjust FSA or HSA contributions
- Change life insurance coverage
- Enroll in dependent care FSA

**Process:**
1. HR sends enrollment materials and deadline (typically Oct 1-15)
2. Employee logs into benefits portal
3. Reviews plan options and coverage
4. Makes elections and confirms
5. Changes take effect Jan 1

## EMPLOYEE TERMINATION AND OFFBOARDING

### Resignation Process

**Employee Resignation:**
1. Employee notifies manager verbally
2. HR notifies director and relevant stakeholders
3. Exit interview scheduled by HR
4. Final paycheck calculated (includes accrued PTO)
5. Return of company property arranged

**Notice Period:**
- **Standard:** Two weeks notice
- **Manager/Executive:** Two weeks to one month
- **At-Will:** No notice required (given voluntarily by employee)

### Involuntary Termination

**Termination Reasons:**
- Violation of code of conduct
- Inability to perform job duties
- Lack of cultural fit (rare, used with caution)
- Reduction in force (layoff due to business needs)
- End of contract (temporary employees)

**Process:**
1. **Documentation:** Performance issues documented over time
2. **Approval:** Legal review and director approval required
3. **Meeting:** Manager and HR present; brief, clear reason given
4. **Separation:** Immediate or notice period (depends on situation)
5. **Severance:** Offered based on length of service and reason

**Severance Packages:**
- **Involuntary/Layoff:** 1 week per year of service (minimum 2 weeks)
- **Reduction in Force:** 1 week per year + 4-week extension
- **Performance Termination:** No severance (rare exception if mutual agreement)

### Offboarding Checklist

**Exit Day Tasks:**
- Collect company badge, keys, equipment
- Change system passwords and access
- Remove from distribution lists and chat channels
- Delete email account (after 30 days)
- Archive employee files
- Update org chart and directory

**Final Paycheck:**
- Accrued PTO paid out
- Earned commission/bonus paid out
- Taxes withheld appropriately
- Final check issued on next payroll date
- Provide last paycheck, W-2, and benefits summary

**Benefits and COBRA:**
- Health benefits end on last day of employment
- COBRA option available for continued health coverage (18-36 months)
- COBRA notice provided at termination
- Employee must elect COBRA within 60 days
- 401(k) plan continues (no changes to account)

**Exit Interview:**
- Conducted by HR (1-on-1, confidential)
- Feedback on employment experience
- Reasons for departure
- Feedback for improvements
- Contact information if future reference needed

### References and Re-hire Eligibility

**Reference Policy:**
- Company provides: Name, dates of employment, job title, rehire status
- Company does not provide: Performance evaluations, salary, specific reasons for termination
- Manager references: At manager discretion (typically name, title, can contact directly)

**Rehire Eligibility:**
- Eligible for rehire: Good/excellent performer who resigned in good standing
- Not eligible: Performance termination, gross misconduct, attendance issues
- Rehire process: Same as new hire (full interview process)

## HR POLICIES AND PROCEDURES

### Confidentiality and Records

**Employee Files - Confidential:**
- Personnel records kept in locked cabinet in HR office
- Employees can request to review their file (24-hour notice)
- Limited access to HR staff only
- 7-year retention after termination

**What's in Personnel File:**
- Offer letter and employment agreement
- Performance reviews and evaluation forms
- Training completion certificates
- Disciplinary action documentation
- Medical leave paperwork
- Benefits elections and changes

### Data Privacy and GDPR Compliance

**Personal Data:**
- Only collected for employment purposes
- Kept secure and confidential
- Not shared with third parties without consent
- Employees can request their data (subject to access form)
- Data retention: 7 years after employment ends

**Compliance:**
- HR policies reviewed annually for compliance
- GDPR training for all HR staff
- Data breach protocol in place
- Privacy notices provided to new hires

### Payroll Procedures

**Payroll Schedule:**
- Bi-weekly (every other Friday)
- 26 pay periods per year
- Holiday payroll: Paid on day before if holiday falls on payday

**Payroll Components:**
- Base salary
- Overtime (if applicable)
- Bonuses and commissions
- PTO payouts
- Deductions: Taxes, 401(k), health insurance, FSA
- Net pay = Gross - All deductions

**Payroll Compliance:**
- Accurate time tracking required
- Overtime authorized by manager
- W-4 forms updated as needed
- Tax withholding checked annually
- I-9 verification within 3 days of hire
