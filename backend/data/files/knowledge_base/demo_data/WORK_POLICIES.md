# Employee Work Policies and Procedures

**Last Updated:** November 2025  
**Effective Date:** November 1, 2025  
**Document ID:** POL-EMP-001

## Overview

This document outlines the standard work policies and procedures for all employees. These policies ensure fair and consistent treatment of all staff members and create a productive, professional workplace environment.

## Working Hours and Schedules

### Standard Work Week
- **Standard Hours:** 40 hours per week (Monday to Friday)
- **Standard Schedule:** 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM with one-hour lunch break
- **Flexible Hours:** Employees may request flexible start/end times with manager approval
- **Core Hours:** 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM (must be present during these times)
- **Overtime:** All overtime must be approved by management in advance

### Time Tracking and Attendance
- Employees must clock in and out using the time tracking system
- Accurate time recording is required for payroll and compliance
- Chronic tardiness or early departure will be addressed through disciplinary action
- Patterns of attendance issues will be documented and may result in performance improvement plans

### Breaks and Lunches
- Employees are entitled to two 15-minute breaks and one 30-minute lunch break daily
- Breaks must be taken during scheduled shift times
- Lunch breaks should not be skipped; coordinate with your team if taking breaks away from desk
- Breaks are not paid time; do not clock out when stepping away briefly

### Attendance Requirements
- Employees are expected to be present and on time every scheduled workday
- Perfect attendance is recognized and appreciated
- Excessive absences may result in disciplinary action up to termination
- Three unexcused absences in a 90-day period may result in disciplinary action

## Leave Policies

### Paid Time Off (PTO)
- **Annual PTO Allocation:** 15 days per calendar year for full-time employees
- **Accrual:** 1.25 days per month for employees hired mid-year
- **Carryover:** Up to 5 days may be carried to the following year; excess is forfeited
- **Payout:** Unused PTO paid at termination or resignation
- **Advance Notice:** Request PTO at least 2 weeks in advance when possible
- **Manager Approval:** All PTO requests must be approved by direct manager
- **Year-End Use:** Managers may require employees to take accrued PTO before December 31

### Sick Leave
- **Allocation:** 8 days per calendar year for full-time employees
- **Use:** For employee or immediate family member illness, medical appointments, or emergencies
- **Documentation:** A doctor's note may be required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days
- **Non-Accruing:** Unused sick days do not carry over to the following year
- **Notification:** Notify manager as soon as possible, ideally before shift starts

### Bereavement Leave
- **Immediate Family:** Up to 5 days for death of spouse, child, parent, or sibling
- **Extended Family:** Up to 3 days for death of grandparent, aunt, uncle, or cousin
- **Verification:** A death certificate or obituary may be required
- **Additional Leave:** Additional unpaid leave may be available upon request
- **Notification:** Notify HR and your manager immediately

### Maternity and Paternity Leave
- **Duration:** Up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- **Additional Paid Leave:** The company provides 4 weeks paid leave for primary caregivers
- **Secondary Caregiver:** Up to 2 weeks paid leave for secondary caregivers
- **Medical Certification:** Medical certification may be required
- **Job Protection:** Employment is protected during approved leave; return to same or equivalent position
- **Notification:** Provide 30 days advance notice when possible

### Jury Duty and Court Appearances
- **Compensation:** Company pays normal wages for up to 5 days of jury duty per calendar year
- **Beyond 5 Days:** Employees continue to receive benefits but not salary beyond 5 days
- **Notification:** Provide a copy of the jury duty summons to HR
- **Schedule Coordination:** Work with your manager to minimize operational impact

## Remote Work Policy

### Eligibility
- Remote work is available for roles where work can be performed outside the office
- Manager and department approval required
- Employees must have satisfactory performance reviews
- Remote work must not impact customer service or team collaboration
- Some roles require on-site presence and are not eligible

### Remote Work Arrangements
- **Hybrid:** 2-3 days per week in office, 2-3 days remote (default arrangement)
- **Full Remote:** Available for approved roles and situations
- **Occasional Remote:** Ad-hoc work from home with manager approval
- **Meetings:** Attendance may be required for critical in-office meetings

### Requirements for Remote Work
- Maintain a professional, distraction-free workspace
- Ensure reliable internet connection and backup connectivity
- Use company-approved VPN and security protocols
- Maintain regular communication with team members
- Be available during core hours (10 AM to 3 PM)
- Participate in video calls when requested
- Secure all sensitive documents and company equipment

### Remote Work Termination
- Either party may terminate remote work arrangements with 2 weeks notice
- Non-compliance with remote work requirements may result in immediate revocation
- Revoked remote work may not be re-requested for 6 months without manager approval

## Equipment and Technology

### Company Equipment
- Laptops, phones, and other equipment remain company property at all times
- Employees are responsible for proper care and use of assigned equipment
- Damage beyond normal wear may result in charges to the employee
- Equipment must be returned within 5 business days of termination
- Failure to return equipment will be reported to collections

### Software and Licenses
- Use only authorized software on company equipment
- Do not install unauthorized applications or software
- License compliance is mandatory; unlicensed software use may result in termination
- Pirated software is strictly prohibited and is a criminal offense
- Report any suspected software license violations to IT

### Personal Device Use
- Personal devices are permitted on the company network with approval
- Personal devices must have up-to-date security and antivirus software
- The company may access company data on personal devices for security purposes
- Personal devices connecting to company network must comply with security policies
- No personal backup of company data to personal cloud storage services

### IT Security and Access
- Protect your login credentials; never share passwords with anyone
- Report suspected security breaches immediately to IT
- Change passwords every 90 days or immediately if compromised
- Do not connect to unsecured public Wi-Fi networks with company data
- Lock your computer when away from your desk
- Do not use company systems for illegal or unethical purposes

## Workplace Communication

### Email Communication
- Use professional language and tone in all emails
- Include clear subject lines and relevant recipients only
- Do not send sensitive information via unsecured email
- Avoid using "Reply to All" unless necessary
- Respond to business emails within 24 business hours
- Archive and delete old emails to maintain system performance

### Instant Messaging and Chat
- Use company chat systems for business communication only
- Maintain professional language and tone
- Do not share sensitive data via unsecured chat channels
- Remember that all chat communications are company property and may be monitored
- Avoid excessive personal chat during work hours

### Phone and Video Calls
- Answer phones professionally and promptly
- Identify yourself and your department when answering business calls
- Keep personal calls brief and minimal during work hours
- Close your office door or use headphones during calls to minimize disruption
- Video calls require appropriate dress and professional appearance

### Social Media
- Do not share confidential company information on personal social media
- Do not represent yourself as speaking for the company on social media
- Be respectful and professional in all online interactions
- Do not engage in online disputes or arguments related to work
- Follow company social media policy when posting about company-related matters

## Health, Safety, and Wellness

### Workplace Safety
- Report all accidents and injuries immediately, no matter how minor
- Follow all safety procedures and use required protective equipment
- Familiarize yourself with emergency procedures and evacuation routes
- Never disable or bypass safety equipment
- Report hazardous conditions to your manager and facilities

### Substance Use at Work
- No use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs at work
- Alcohol consumption only at authorized company events
- Reporting to work under the influence is prohibited
- Violation of substance policies may result in immediate termination
- The company may require drug testing as permitted by law

### Mental Health and Wellness
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) available for free confidential counseling
- Mental health resources available through benefits information
- Discuss mental health accommodations with HR
- Stigma-free workplace for mental health discussions
- Manager support for employees seeking wellness resources

### Workplace Ergonomics
- Workstations are evaluated for proper ergonomic setup
- Report ergonomic issues to HR or Facilities
- Adjustable equipment available upon request
- Training provided on proper posture and ergonomic practices
- Preventive measures help reduce workplace injuries

## Performance and Development

### Performance Expectations
- All employees are expected to meet role-specific performance standards
- Performance is evaluated annually or more frequently if needed
- Goals and expectations are discussed with manager regularly
- Continuous improvement is encouraged and supported
- Underperformance will be addressed through performance improvement plans

### Performance Reviews
- Formal reviews conducted annually, typically in your hire month
- Mid-year check-ins scheduled to discuss progress
- Self-assessments are part of the review process
- 360-degree feedback may be collected from colleagues
- Review results documented and placed in personnel file

### Training and Development
- Budget available for job-related training and professional development
- Tuition reimbursement available for degree-related courses
- Professional conferences and seminars encouraged
- Internal training programs offered throughout the year
- Discuss development opportunities with your manager

## Dress Code

### General Guidelines
- Business casual dress code required for most positions
- Client-facing roles: Business formal attire expected
- Remote days: Professional attire still required for video meetings
- Seasonal exceptions: Casual Fridays for most departments (check with your manager)

### Business Casual Standards
- Collared shirts, blouses, or sweaters
- Dress pants, khakis, or professional skirts/dresses
- Closed-toe shoes with minimal heel
- Clean, neat appearance and grooming

### Business Formal Standards
- Button-down shirts or blouses
- Dress pants, formal skirts, or dresses
- Jackets or blazers (may be required)
- Dress shoes and minimal jewelry

### What is Not Appropriate
- Jeans, torn or distressed clothing
- Athletic wear or activewear
- T-shirts with offensive language or images
- Tank tops, halter tops, or excessively revealing clothing
- Flip-flops or athletic shoes
- Excessive perfume or strong scents

## Travel and Expenses

### Expense Reimbursement
- All business-related expenses must be pre-approved by manager
- Receipts required for all expenses over $25
- Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of incurrence
- Reimbursement processed within 1-2 pay periods after submission
- Fraudulent expenses will result in disciplinary action

### Travel Policy
- Book travel through company-approved travel vendors when possible
- Economy class required for flights under 6 hours
- Business class may be approved for international flights over 6 hours
- Hotel accommodations: 3-star or equivalent unless prior approval
- Per diem rates: See expense policy for daily meal allowances

### Business Meals
- Business meals must have a legitimate business purpose
- Meals for employees should not exceed $20 per person
- Client entertainment: Pre-approval required for meals exceeding $100
- Alcoholic beverages: Maximum 2 drinks per person at business meals
- Include attendees and business purpose on expense report

## Performance Improvement and Termination

### Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)
- Formal plan developed for employees with performance concerns
- Clear objectives, timelines, and expectations outlined
- Regular check-ins with manager (typically weekly or bi-weekly)
- Duration typically 30-90 days depending on severity
- Failure to meet PIP objectives may result in termination

### At-Will Employment
- Employment is at-will: Either party may terminate employment at any time
- Termination may be without cause and without notice period
- Two weeks notice is requested but not required for resignations
- Outstanding PTO paid at termination (if required by law)

### Exit Procedures
- Employees must return all company property before final paycheck
- Benefits continuation information provided (COBRA, etc.)
- References: Company provides job title and employment dates only
- Final paycheck includes pay through last day and accrued PTO
- Exit interview may be conducted

## Acknowledgment

All employees are required to acknowledge receipt and understanding of this policy. Failure to comply with these policies may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
